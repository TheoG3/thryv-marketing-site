# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Miami small and midsize business owners: contractors, restaurateurs, dentists, medspas, immigration lawyers, beauty salons. They've often been burned by agencies that overpromised and underdelivered. They're skeptical of slick sales decks and generic marketing speak. They want to know exactly what they're getting, what it costs, and whether it will actually work for their market — not "businesses like theirs" somewhere else.

Secondary: Miami nonprofits seeking grant-funder visibility and community trust. Marketing agencies shopping for a white-label partner.

Context: Usually on a laptop, mid-workday, comparing 2-3 options. Decision timeline of 1-2 weeks. Bilingual audience — roughly 30% will be more comfortable in Spanish.

## Product Purpose

Thryv Marketing Solutions is a Miami-based digital marketing agency offering GBP management, reputation management, local SEO/AEO/GEO, website builds, Google Ads, and social media ads, plus consulting engagements for owners who want a roadmap rather than a retainer.

The site exists to convert visitors into clients — and to prove, through its own quality, that Thryv can actually do what it claims to do. The site is the portfolio. A marketing agency's website IS its best case study.

Success: a business owner lands on any page and within 30 seconds understands what Thryv does, believes it's the right fit for Miami, and knows exactly how to take the next step.

## Brand Personality

Confident, direct, grounded. The voice of someone who has worked this market long enough to skip the pitch and just tell you what works. Not arrogant — earned. Not aggressive — clear. Not corporate — local.

Emotional goal: the visitor should feel like they finally found someone who gets it. Relief, not excitement.

## Anti-References

- **Generic SaaS marketing** (HubSpot, Semrush, Mailchimp): corporate blues, stock photography, gradient hero orbs, "platform for growth" non-language, features presented as benefits.
- **Cheap local business sites**: bad stock photos, visual clutter, pricing tables that look like spreadsheets, zero typographic intention.
- **AI-template sites**: identical card grids with icon + heading + 2-line text, glassmorphism, gradient text, every section using the same spacing and layout rhythm, shimmer effects everywhere.
- **Agency brag sites**: awards sections, "we've helped 500+ brands" without specifics, client logo carousels, case study decks that tell you nothing.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the case study.** A marketing agency that can't market itself loses before the conversation starts. Every page must demonstrate the craft we're selling — typography, copy, hierarchy, pace.
2. **Earned confidence, not performed confidence.** No fake social proof, no fabricated metrics, no urgency tricks. Authority comes from specificity: knowing Miami neighborhoods, knowing local search mechanics, using real numbers with sources.
3. **Zero filler.** No section exists just to fill space. No heading that restates the heading above it. No bullet point that says the same thing as the previous one in different words.
4. **Local precision is the differentiator.** National agencies know "local SEO." We know Overtown, Little Haiti, Brickell, and what searches convert in each. That specificity should show up in copy, examples, and context — not just in claims.
5. **Show the work, don't narrate it.** Don't write "results-driven" — demonstrate it. Don't write "we pay attention to detail" — pay attention to detail. The interface itself is the argument.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum across all pages.
- Bilingual: English primary, Spanish trust signals and content where indicated (Miami market — roughly 30% Spanish-dominant).
- Reduced motion: all animations must respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`.
- Color contrast: 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components.
- No color as sole differentiator — hover, focus, and state changes must shift shape or label, not just color.
